# Nouns, adjectives and articles

## Parts of speech

- Noun: person, place, thing, or idea (e.g., cat, city, love)
- Adjective: describes a noun (e.g., big, red, beautiful)
- Article: defines a noun as specific or unspecific (e.g., a, an, the)

## Agreement

Nouns, articles, and adjectives need to match:
- gender
- number

Feminine nouns often end in:

- -a (e.g., niña, casa)
- -ad (e.g., ciudad, libertad)
- -z (e.g., luz, voz)
- -ión (e.g., canción, lección)

Masculine nouns often end in:

- -o (e.g., gato, libro)
- -e (e.g., coche, puente)
- -l (e.g., papel, árbol)
- -aje (e.g., viaje, garaje)
- -n (e.g., pan, jardín)
- -r (e.g., color, amor)

A group of males and females is masculine.

## Plurals

Noun ends in a vowel? Add -s for plural (e.g., gato → gatos)

Noun ends in a consonant? Add -es for plural (e.g., papel → papeles)

## Articles

Gives context to a noun.

"La casa" (the house) - specific

"Una casa" (a house) - unspecific

Article has to match gender and number of noun.

### Definite article

> Noun is specific

- el
- la
- los
- las

### Indefinite article

> Noun is *not* specific

- un
- una
- unos
- unas

## Adjectives

Adjectives have to match gender and number of noun.

> The small house (article + adjective + noun)

> La casa pequeña (aritcle + noun + adjective)


### Dynamic Gender and Number

- pequeueña/o ; pequeñas/os
- blanca/o ; blancas/os
- bueno/a ; buenos/as

> El libro pequeño

> La casa pequeña

### Static Gender, Dynamic Number

- grande ; grandes
- verde ; verdes
- inteligente ; inteligentes
- azul ; azules

### Number and Gender Signs

- Plural: -s/-es
- Gender: -a/-o

# Ser y Estar

## Only Ser is correct

###  "What or who something is"

- Es mi hermano (He is my brother)
- Es una araña (It is a spider)
- Somos familia (We are family)
- Somos amigos (We are friends)

## Only Estar is correct

### "Where is something or someone?"

- (The family is all here) La familia está toda aquí
- (The house is near the park) La casa está cerca del parque.
- (The car is in the garage) El coche está en el garaje

### "What is someone doing?"

- (They are talking) Están hablando.
- (We are eating) Estamos comiendo.
- (He is playing) Está jugando.
- (He is playing outside) Está jugando afuera.
- (He is playing in his room) Está jugando en su cuarto.

## Both Ser and Estar **could be**  correct

### Estar for feelings, states, conditions

- (My wife is happy today) Mi esposa está feliz hoy.
- (I'm happy today because I have the day off) Estoy feliz hoy porque tengo el día libre.
- (The house is dirty) La casa está sucia.

### Ser for **distinctive features and characteristics** 

- (My friend is good) Mi amiga es buena.
- (The house is pretty) La casa es bonita.

# Hay

- There is / There are
- Used to indicate the existence of something.

Example:

> There is a dog (Hay un perro)

> There are two dogs (Hay dos perros)

## Que

- A "linker"
- MANDATORY

Example:

> Hoy estamos en la capital **que** está en el centro del país.

> (Today we are in the capital **which** is in the center of the country.)

## Intonation

- Goes up for "closed-ended" questions (yes/no)
- Makes a triangle shape for "open-ended" questions (what, where, why, how, when) (same as English)

## Regular vs Irregular verbs

- Regular verbs follow a consistent pattern when conjugated.
- Irregular verbs do not follow these patterns and have unique conjugations.

- Example regular verbs:
    - hablar (to speak)
    - comer (to eat)
    - vivir (to live)

### Regular verb

- hablar (to speak)
  - Yo hablo (I speak)
  - Tú hablas (You speak)
  - Él/Ella habla (He/She speaks)
  - Nosotros hablamos (We speak)
  - Ellos/Ellas hablan (They speak)

### Irregular verb

- ser (to be)
  - Yo soy (I am)
  - Tú eres (You are)
  - Él/Ella es (He/She is)
  - Nosotros somos (We are)
  - Ellos/Ellas son (They are)

## Conjugation endings

- tu - always (one exception) end in `-s` (estás, hablas, comes, vives)
- ustedes (you all), ellos/as (they). Alwas end in `-n` (hablan, comen, viven)
- nosotros/as (we) - always end in `-mos` (hablamos, comemos, vivimos)

## Regular Patterns

"Yo" form present tense always end in `-o` (hablo, como, vivo)
